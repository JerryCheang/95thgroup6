95th School Anniversary Project

Designing the Future Block D Building: A Sustainable School Campus Vision - Verdant Link Hall

St. Joseph Diocesan College (The Fifth School)

Danny, Alexander, Kenny, Sophie, Jason

1. Executive Summary

This proposal explains the design for the new Block D, which focuses on sustainable development and smart functions. The project's goal is to create a multi-use space that cares about the environment, is accessible to everyone, and supports the community's well-being.

The design includes key features such as solar panels to convert sunlight into energy, rainwater-harvesting systems to conserve water, and green roofs planted with vegetation. These help the building comply with modern environmental protection standards. At the same time, the building encourages diversity and learning.

This design is unique because it combines green technology with spaces for art, recreation, and education, helping reduce environmental impact and promote creative growth for everyone.

2. Design Concept & SDG Integration

Design Vision

Create an eco-friendly, practical, and modern-styled building.

Provide an accessible space that supports the environment and benefits the community.

Sustainable Design

  • Solar panels generate renewable energy.
  • Rainwater collection conserves water resources.
  • Green roofs improve air quality and energy efficiency.
  • Meets modern environmental standards.

Learning & Community Spaces

  • Dedicated areas for art, entertainment, and learning.
  • Encourages creativity and educational development.

SDG 3 - Good Health and Well-being

The entire building encourages a healthy lifestyle through fresh-air ventilation, natural light, and plenty of indoor and outdoor spaces for exercise and relaxation. With relaxation zones like garden seating and sunshade canopies, students have a peaceful retreat from academic stress. Shower rooms and changing areas promote hygiene among participants in sports, dance, or other after-school activities.

SDG 4 - Quality Education

Block D is a modern learning platform that supports creative, academic, and social growth. Art studios, dance rooms, flexible multi-purpose spaces, and self-study rooms promote independence and collaboration. Accessibility throughout ensures that all individuals can fully participate in the educational experience.

SDG 7 - Affordable and Clean Energy

Solar panels on the roof, paired with energy-saving appliances throughout, allow Block D to generate and consume clean energy, reducing both costs and carbon emissions. The building's design and advanced insulation minimize the need for artificial heating, cooling, and lighting.

SDG 11 - Sustainable Cities and Communities

The building promotes inclusive access, safe circulation, and community engagement by featuring accessible lifts, fire-safe stairs, and barrier-free ramps. Common spaces, such as the roofed playground and gardens, reinforce a nurturing, interactive environment.

SDG 12 - Responsible Consumption and Production

We use locally sourced building materials and efficient energy systems, such as solar panels, rainwater collectors, and energy-saving lighting, to minimize resource waste. Storage solutions and recycling points are embedded throughout the facility to encourage mindful consumption.

SDG 13 - Climate Action

Climate-resilient infrastructure is key: rooftop solar arrays, extensive greening (green walls and roof gardens), and a rainwater collection system reduce the carbon footprint and adapt the structure to local weather extremes.

3. Floor Plan & Functional Layout

The spatial arrangement connects sustainability with daily campus activities.

Exterior Design Notes

Modern architectural style with clean, straight lines and a minimalist aesthetic.

Grey concrete with large glass window panels arranged in a symmetrical grid pattern.

Vertical narrow strips next to windows to help the glass last longer.

Ground Floor (G/F)

  • Welcoming lobby and access to a roofed playground, ensuring shelter from the weather.
  • Parcel and food delivery locker system for modern convenience.

First Floor (1/F)

  • Dedicated dancing rooms for performing arts.
  • Changing and shower rooms for hygiene and privacy.
  • Prop storage room for costumes and equipment.

Second Floor (2/F)

  • Specialised art rooms for various media.
  • Art material storage, including secure areas for paints.
  • Display area for showcasing student artwork.

Third Floor (3/F)

  • Self-study rooms supporting focused learning.
  • Multi-purpose rooms accommodating events, workshops, or meetings.
  • Document storage cabinets for organisational needs.

Fourth Floor (4/F) - Roof

  • Lush garden with seating and sunshade canopy for relaxation and outdoor learning.
  • Integrated rainwater collection system for irrigation.
  • Flat rooftop with green space, including landscaping and plant beds, indicating the use of rooftop gardens.
  • Solar panels are installed on the rooftop on one side, suggesting an emphasis on renewable energy and sustainability.

Interiors

  • Two lifts (>=1000kg).
  • Clear corridor signage on every floor improves navigation and accessibility.
  • Extensive firefighting systems include extinguishers, fire stairs, and smoke detectors.
  • Wheelchair ramps and other disabled-friendly facilities.
  • Water dispensers are placed on every floor to provide clean drinking water.
  • Recycling bins on every floor improve recycling efficiency.

4. Sustainability Features

  • Solar Panels & Sun-tracking System: Generate renewable energy and reduce operating costs.
  • Rainwater Collection System: Collect and reuse rainwater for landscape irrigation.
  • Natural Ventilation & Lighting: Window placement maximises daylight and cross-ventilation, lowering energy needs.
  • Green Roof Garden: Insulates building, promotes biodiversity, and creates leisure space.

5. Data & Survey Findings

Pie Chart 1: Total Energy Consumption in the United States (2020)

This chart shows that solar energy accounts for only 1% among all common energy sources. This suggests that solar panels have strong development potential because they require minimal operating energy.

Considering the climate and surroundings of Block E, installing rooftop solar panels is the most suitable renewable energy solution. Macau receives sufficient sunlight, especially during summer, making rooftop installation ideal without occupying indoor building space.

Bar Chart 1: Average Monthly Sunshine in Macau (1990-2020)

The data shows adequate sunlight conditions throughout the year in Macau, supporting the effectiveness of solar energy systems installed on the rooftop.

Pie Chart 2: Frequency of Using Food Delivery Services in Malaysia

The chart indicates that most people use food delivery services 1-3 times per week. This suggests that students and teachers may also order meals during school hours.

Therefore, a monitored reception area with food delivery lockers is proposed for Block E. This improves convenience during lunch hours and prevents food accumulation and misunderstandings caused by disorder.

Bar Chart 2: Average Monthly Rainfall in Macau (1990-2020)

The chart shows peak rainfall in June and August at approximately 320-330 mm, while May, July, and September also record high rainfall levels.

This indicates strong potential for rainwater collection. Rainwater gathered from the fourth-floor rooftop can be filtered and reused for purposes such as watering campus plants, supporting eco-friendly practices and environmental education.

6. Reflection & Team Roles

Our group aimed to design a building that is both eco-friendly and supportive of student activities. We observed that club practices, such as dancing and jump rope, were held in crowded areas near the tuck shop, while many students also lacked quiet personal spaces for studying.

These challenges inspired us to redesign Block D by introducing:

  • Dedicated dance and activity spaces.
  • Comfortable self-study areas.
  • A more flexible and student-friendly learning environment.

Challenges Faced

During the project, we encountered several difficulties:

  • Generating ideal floor plans using AI tools.
  • Balancing different facility needs.
  • Maintaining effective communication within the team.

To address these challenges, we refined our designs repeatedly and avoided including unsustainable facilities, such as energy-intensive sports areas.

Skills Learned

Through this project, we developed:

  • Floor planning and architectural design knowledge.
  • Research skills for community-friendly building design.
  • Teamwork and communication abilities.
  • Deeper understanding of sustainable design concepts.

Integrating sustainability into architecture allowed us to contribute ideas toward a more eco-friendly campus environment.
